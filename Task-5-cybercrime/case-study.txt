Case Study (Phishing Example)

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords or bank details. 
It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate.

For example, a student may receive an email claiming to be from a bank, asking them to verify their account details. 
The link in the email redirects to a fake website that looks real. 
When the student enters their details, the attacker gains access to their account.

Students and inexperienced users are often targeted because they may not recognize such scams.
The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and data misuse.

Therefore,
awareness and caution are essential to prevent phishing attacks.
